Program in its making

Order:

  1. High level plan of the desired computation
  2. High level control flow of overall computation abstracting complex details. e.g., by introducing sub-routines etc.
  3. Decide on data structures to handle information during computation.
  4. Fill in other details in overall computation.
  5. Implement sub-routines.

2. High level control flow of overall computation

While writing high level control flow, I have experienced that it is often helpful to jump around the control flow. For
example, I find it natural to jump directly to the major part(s) of the control flow in my overall computation, ignoring
other insignificant details. It helps in a way, to give priority to major control flow(s) and putting other parts
around it.

Example of a program: If I want to do some computation X() for many combinations using some nested loops. In such case,
while writing high level control flow, I will start with high level control flow of X(), leaving out complex details
as sub-routines. Once control flow for X() is defined, I will start thinking how to put loops to do X() for required
combinations.
